Blackened Catfish

This Creole-style blackened catfish is bursting with bold, smoky flavors and a rich, spicy crust. Seared to perfection in a cast-iron skillet, it's served with a fresh squeeze of lemon and pairs beautifully with Dirty Rice, Maque Choux or a classic Remoulade.

Ingredients:

  • 4 catfish fillets
  • 4 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • lemon wedges for garnish
  • fresh parsely for garnish

For Blackening Seasoning:

  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp salt

Directions:

  1. Mix all blackening seasonings in a bowl and set aside.
  2. Pat catfish fillets dry with a paper towel.
  3. Brush sides of fish with melted butter.
  4. Coat the fillets evenly with blackening seasoning.
  5. Heat a cast iron skillet over high heat until its smoking hot.
  6. Add vegetable oil to the pan and then add the catfish.
  7. Cook 2-3 minutes per side until a dark, crispy crust forms.
  8. Squeeze lemon juice over the fish.
  9. Garnish with parsely and a lemon wedge.

Serve with a Remoulade for an extra burst of flavor!
